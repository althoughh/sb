01 Challenge

Delivering value without disrupting the roadmap

As a high-growth portfolio company, Copper was balancing rapid product development with increasing cloud infrastructure costs. Their AWS spend had grown to over $1.9 million annually, but a significant portion of compute was still being charged at undiscounted, on-demand rates.

The investment thesis depended on sustained margin expansion, but optimizing cloud spend traditionally required engineering time that Copper could not spare from their product roadmap. Previous attempts at DIY commitment management had created rigid vendor lock-in without delivering proportional savings.

Copper needed a partner who could deliver immediate bottom-line value, require zero technical changes, preserve technical and financial flexibility, and provide visibility suitable for board-level reporting.

02 Solution

Portfolio-wide FinOps with zero engineering effort

Strategic Blue deployed their non-intrusive AWS FinOps Assessment, requiring no access to Copper's workloads or application data. The assessment identified immediate savings opportunities across eligible services including EC2, Fargate, Lambda, and RDS.

Through a careful blend of Reserved Instances and Savings Plans managed in dedicated accounts, Strategic Blue achieved 31% savings on eligible services while maintaining 100% commitment utilization. Crucially, this was delivered without any technical changes to Copper's infrastructure.

Strategic Blue's approach avoided the rigid multi-year commitments that typically come with DIY optimization, giving Copper the financial and technical flexibility to evolve their architecture as the business scaled.

03 Results

$227k EBITDA expansion, sustained over 12 months

Over the last 12 months, Strategic Blue delivered $227k in direct EBITDA expansion for Copper on their AWS usage. This was achieved through a 31% net savings rate on eligible services and 100% commitment utilization, with savings applied directly to the invoice.

The cost savings achieved have freed up budget and enabled Copper to prioritize other key parts of their business, from product development to customer acquisition. The engineering team remained focused on the product roadmap throughout the partnership, with zero hours spent on cost optimization.

As Copper continues to scale, Strategic Blue's proactive approach to identifying new savings opportunities will continue to play a key role in sustained margin expansion and portfolio-level value creation.
